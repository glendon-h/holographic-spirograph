Shape A — outer fixed shape. The track that B rolls inside.
Shape B — inner rolling shape. Rolls along the inside of A without slipping.
Contact point — where B touches A. Moves along A's perimeter.
Center of B — offset inward from the contact point by B's local radius.
Pen arm — line from B's center to the pen tip. Length = pen offset × size ratio.
Pen point — the tip that draws the spirograph. Attached to B, rotates as B rolls.
Trace — the path drawn by the pen point. This is the spirograph.
The spirograph is produced by the rolling constraint: B rolls along A's perimeter with no slipping. The distance traveled on A equals the distance traveled on B. The pen point is fixed to B's body, so as B rotates, the pen traces a complex curve.
